1. Data Sources

MOT Test Data (Primary Source)

The UK's MOT (Ministry of Transport) test is required annually for vehicles over 3 years old. The DVSA (Driver and Vehicle Standards Agency) provides bulk access to historical MOT data through their API.

  • Some early-year fleet size estimates are interpolated between confirmed data points
  • Confirmed data points: 2019 (12.0M diesel cars) and 2024 (8.3M diesel cars) from GOV.UK

2. Vehicle Survival Model

Definition

Survival rate is the proportion of vehicles originally registered in a given year that are still actively on the road (defined as having a valid MOT in 2024-2025).

Calculation Method

For each registration year (2005-2020):
  total_registered = COUNT DISTINCT vehicles with fuelType='DIESEL'
  still_active = COUNT DISTINCT vehicles with MOT in 2024 or 2025
  survival_rate = still_active / total_registered

Results

Key Insight

The survival curve shows that diesel vehicles have a roughly 15-year "half-life" - by age 15, only about 66% remain. This is critical for predicting future fleet size, as the peak diesel cohorts (2015-2017) will begin significant attrition from 2030 onwards.

3. Annual Mileage Model

Calculation Method

Annual mileage is calculated from consecutive MOT test odometer readings:

annual_miles = (odometer_reading_2 - odometer_reading_1) / years_between_tests * 365

To ensure accuracy, we filtered to:

  • Test gaps of 300-450 days (approximately 1 year)
  • Positive mileage increases only
  • Mileage increases under 50,000 miles (to exclude data errors)

Mileage Decline Over Time

We also found that diesels are being driven less than they used to be. Comparing 5-year-old diesels:

  • 2015: Average 60,700 miles at MOT
  • 2024: Average 50,400 miles at MOT
  • Decline: 17%

This likely reflects increased working from home post-pandemic, higher fuel costs, and owners keeping diesels as second cars for occasional use.

4. Consumption Model

Formula

For each year (2020-2040):
  For each vehicle cohort (by registration year):
    surviving_vehicles = original_sales × survival_rate(age)
    annual_miles = mileage_by_age(age)
    mpg = fuel_economy_by_age(age)

    litres = (surviving_vehicles × annual_miles) / mpg × 4.546

  total_consumption = SUM(litres for all cohorts)

5. Model Validation

Backtest Against Historical Data

We tested the model against known fuel consumption data from 2015-2024:

*Error calculated assuming cars+vans are ~55% of total road diesel (remainder is HGVs, buses).

Validation Result

The model predictions are within ±15% of the benchmark for most years. The 2020 over-prediction reflects the model not accounting for COVID-19 lockdowns. Overall, the model provides a reasonable basis for future projections.

6. Limitations

Known Limitations

  • Cars only: This analysis covers diesel passenger cars. Vans (which are 85% diesel) and HGVs are modeled separately with less precision.
  • No regional breakdown: Rural areas with fewer EV charging options may retain diesel longer.
  • Policy uncertainty: Future ULEZ expansions, tax changes, or bans could accelerate decline.
  • Van data simplified: Van fleet modeling uses aggregate assumptions rather than detailed cohort analysis.

Sensitivity Analysis

The interactive explorer on the main page allows users to adjust key assumptions and see the impact on predictions. Key sensitivities:

  • ±10% change in survival rates shifts 2035 consumption by approximately ±0.2B litres
  • ±5% change in annual mileage decline shifts 2035 consumption by approximately ±0.3B litres
